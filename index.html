2026: Year of Midwest Quantum

A community-led narrative to align, amplify, and celebrate the Midwest’s quantum momentum.

Overview

2026 is shaping up to be a defining year for quantum in the Midwest. With global attention on the Midwest driven by technological advances, investment, major conferences, and international companies establishing a presence here, 2026 presents a unique opportunity to build on the momentum created by our quantum community.

Across research, startups, enterprise adoption, workforce development, and global convening, an unprecedented concentration of quantum activity is coincidentally taking place in the region. The Year of Midwest Quantum is a community-led narrative designed to align, amplify, and celebrate this momentum under a shared banner.

This is not a new organization, conference, or governing body. It is a unifying story that reflects what is already happening and gives the ecosystem a common language to tell it together.

Why the Midwest

The Midwest has quietly become one of the most complete quantum ecosystems in the world.

The Midwest uniquely combines

  • World-class academic and national lab research
  • A growing base of quantum startups
  • International quantum companies planting flags
  • Strong enterprise and cloud participation
  • Public-private investment and infrastructure
  • A dense calendar of global quantum conferences

Purpose

The Year of Midwest Quantum exists to

  • Align the ecosystem around a shared narrative
  • Increase global visibility for Midwest-based quantum work
  • Maintain momentum from the International Year of Quantum in 2025
  • Connect industry, academia, government, and investors
  • Attract talent, capital, and partnerships to the region
  • Create continuity across conferences and initiatives

Core Principles

Neutral and non-competitive
Amplifies existing efforts rather than replacing them
Low-friction participation
Focused on long-term ecosystem impact

Proposed Events/Workshops

Year of Midwest Quantum Kickoff

Timing: February or March 2026

A community kickoff event to formally open the Year of Midwest Quantum and align stakeholders at the start of the year.

Objectives

  • Set the shared narrative and goals for the year
  • Highlight ongoing initiatives already underway
  • Connect leaders across academia, industry, startups, and government
  • Announce the full 2026 event calendar

Proposed format

  • Half-day or evening program
  • Short keynote remarks from ecosystem anchors
  • Lightning talks from startups, labs, and enterprise partners
  • Networking reception

Potential hosts/venues: University campus or national lab, mHUB, downtown venue.

Quantum Qoffee’s

Cadence: Monthly, Mar–Dec 2026

Build consistent community engagement, strengthen connections, and maintain momentum between major conferences and events.

Objectives

  • Open networking
  • One short spotlight talk or fireside chat
  • Optional lightning updates from the community

Rotating hosts: Universities, innovation hubs, and partner spaces, with conference-adjacent sessions when stakeholders are in town.

Year of Midwest Quantum Closing Event

Timing: December 2026

A capstone gathering to reflect on the year, share outcomes, and set momentum for what comes next.

Objectives

  • Celebrate community achievements and collaborations
  • Highlight research, pilots, and commercial progress
  • Capture lessons learned and ecosystem growth
  • Signal continuity beyond 2026

Proposed format: Evening reception or half-day forum.

Timing considerations: Align with December conferences or Q2B.
